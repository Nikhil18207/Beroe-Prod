# Volume Bundling Opportunity

## Overview
Consolidate spend across suppliers and regions to achieve volume discounts and negotiate better pricing through increased leverage.

## Context
The Volume Bundling micro-agent evaluates proof points with the hypothesis that **concentration and fragmentation patterns indicate bundling opportunities**.

## Proof Points (8)

| Code | Name | Context for Volume Bundling |
|------|------|----------------------------|
| PP_REGIONAL_SPEND | Regional Spend Addressability | High concentration = GOOD (can consolidate across sites) |
| PP_TAIL_SPEND | Tail Spend Consolidation | High tail spend = GOOD (opportunity to consolidate) |
| PP_VOLUME_LEVERAGE | Volume Leverage from Fragmented Spend | Fragmented = GOOD (opportunity to leverage) |
| PP_PRICE_VARIANCE | Price Variance for Identical SKUs | High variance = negotiate standardized volume pricing |
| PP_AVG_SPEND_SUPPLIER | Average Spend per Supplier | Low average = too many suppliers = consolidation needed |
| PP_MARKET_CONSOLIDATION | Market Consolidation (HHI) | Low HHI = competitive market = buyer has leverage |
| PP_SUPPLIER_LOCATION | Supplier Location | Same region suppliers = logistics bundling opportunity |
| PP_SUPPLIER_RISK_RATING | Supplier Risk Rating | Low risk suppliers = safe to consolidate with |

## Savings Benchmark
- **Low**: 0% (Initiative 1)
- **High**: 5% (Initiative 2)
- **Addressable Spend**: 80%

## Example Insights
- "Regional concentration at 95% in India/Malaysia enables cross-site volume consolidation"
- "Tail spend of 28% across 45 suppliers presents significant consolidation opportunity"
- "Spend fragmented across 23 suppliers - top supplier has only 12%"

## Key Difference from Other Opportunities
Same data, different interpretation:
- **Volume Bundling**: Price variance = "consolidate and standardize pricing"
- **Target Pricing**: Price variance = "use lowest price as negotiation target"
