# Risk Management Opportunity

## Overview
Identify supply chain vulnerabilities and develop mitigation strategies for supplier, geographic, financial, and market risks.

## Context
The Risk Management micro-agent evaluates proof points with the hypothesis that **concentration and external factors create supply chain vulnerabilities**.

## Proof Points (7)

| Code | Name | Context for Risk Management |
|------|------|----------------------------|
| PP_SINGLE_SOURCING | Single Sourcing / Supplier Dependency | High dependency = HIGH RISK, need backups |
| PP_SUPPLIER_CONCENTRATION | Supplier Concentration Risk | High concentration = supply chain vulnerability |
| PP_CATEGORY_RISK | Category Risk | Inherent category volatility and supply constraints |
| PP_INFLATION | Inflation | High inflation = need price escalation clauses |
| PP_EXCHANGE_RATE | Exchange Rate | Multi-currency = need hedging |
| PP_GEO_POLITICAL | Geo Political Risk | High-risk regions = diversification needed |
| PP_SUPPLIER_RISK_RATING | Supplier Risk Rating | High-risk suppliers = replacement needed |

## Savings Benchmark
- **Low**: 1% (cost avoidance)
- **High**: 3% (cost avoidance)
- **Impact Type**: Risk Reduction

## Example Insights
- "Single-source dependency is critical: 65% of items have only 1 supplier - diversification recommended"
- "Supply base is highly concentrated (HHI: 3200) - top supplier holds 52% of spend"
- "Category inherent risk is high - key factors: supply constraints, geopolitical sensitivity"
- "Inflation exposure is high at 12.5% weighted average - implement price escalation clauses"
- "45% of spend from geopolitically sensitive regions: Russia, China, Turkey"

## Key Difference from Other Opportunities
Same data, different interpretation:
- **Volume Bundling**: Supplier Risk Rating = "low-risk suppliers are safe to consolidate with"
- **Risk Management**: Supplier Risk Rating = "high-risk suppliers need replacement"
